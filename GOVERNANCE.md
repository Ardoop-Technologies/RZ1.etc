# GOVERNANCE.md – Governance Model

**Ethical AI MY – Non-Centralized Governance Structure**

---

## Overview

This document describes the governance model enabling ethical AI development in Malaysia. The model is fundamentally **non-centralized**, emphasizing distributed autonomy while maintaining alignment through shared ethical principles and transparent processes.

---

## Core Governance Philosophy

### Non-Centralized Decision-Making

The model enables different stakeholders to make independent decisions while maintaining alignment through:

- Shared ethical principles – Common values guide decisions
- Transparent communication – Open information flow
- Mutual accountability – Stakeholders hold each other accountable
- Collaborative problem-solving – Collective engagement on conflicts

### Autonomy Within Alignment

Stakeholders have autonomous decision-making authority within their context, bounded by ethical principles. No central authority mandates decisions; instead, alignment emerges from shared commitment to ethical principles.

---

## Stakeholder Roles

### Organizations
**Role:** Develop and deploy AI systems ethically.

**Responsibilities:**
- Adopt and implement ethical framework
- Conduct impact assessments
- Maintain transparent documentation
- Establish internal governance
- Report on outcomes
- Engage with community feedback

### Development Teams
**Role:** Design and build ethical AI systems.

**Responsibilities:**
- Understand ethical requirements
- Implement technical safeguards
- Document design decisions
- Conduct testing and validation
- Maintain code quality
- Support transparency

### Research Institutions
**Role:** Advance AI ethics understanding.

**Responsibilities:**
- Conduct ethical research
- Share findings with community
- Validate frameworks
- Mentor practitioners
- Contribute to policy development
- Maintain academic rigor

### Government and Regulatory Bodies
**Role:** Establish legal frameworks.

**Responsibilities:**
- Develop regulatory requirements
- Provide legal guidance
- Enforce compliance
- Engage with stakeholders
- Support capacity building

### Communities and Civil Society
**Role:** Advocate community interests.

**Responsibilities:**
- Represent community perspectives
- Report harms and concerns
- Advocate for protections
- Provide feedback
- Hold stakeholders accountable

## Governance Mechanisms

### 1. Transparency and Documentation
**Mechanism:** All AI systems and governance decisions documented and available for review.

### 2. Ethical Review and Assessment
**Mechanism:** Structured evaluation against ethical principles before deployment.

### 3. Community Engagement
**Mechanism:** Opportunities for affected parties to provide input and feedback.

### 4. Escalation and Conflict Resolution
**Mechanism:** Processes for addressing disagreements constructively.

### 5. Mutual Accountability
**Mechanism:** Stakeholders hold each other accountable through review and feedback.

---

## Decision-Making Framework

### For Individual Organizations

**Process:**
1. Identify decision and affected parties
2. Assess against ethical principles
3. Consult relevant stakeholders
4. Document rationale and constraints
5. Make decision with accountability
6. Implement and monitor
7. Report outcomes

### For Cross-Stakeholder Issues

**Process:**
1. Convene affected stakeholders
2. Share information and perspectives
3. Seek common ground
4. Develop collaborative solutions
5. Document agreements
6. Implement with flexibility
7. Monitor and adapt

---

## Principles of Good Governance

- **Transparency** – Open processes and decisions
- **Accountability** – Clear responsibility and redress
- **Inclusivity** – All affected stakeholders participate
- **Fairness** – Equitable and respectful processes
- **Effectiveness** – Achieves intended objectives
- **Flexibility** – Adapts to changing context

---

## Implementation Guidance

Organizations implementing this model should:

1. Establish governance structures
2. Create documentation practices
3. Develop engagement processes
4. Build stakeholder capacity
5. Monitor outcomes
6. Report transparently
7. Continuously improve
