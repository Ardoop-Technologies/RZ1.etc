# ETHICS.md – Ethical Framework

**Ethical AI MY – Core Principles and Values**

---

## Overview

This document establishes the fundamental ethical principles guiding AI development, deployment, and governance in Malaysia. These principles are offered as a reference framework and represent shared values that stakeholders are encouraged to adopt and adapt according to their context.

---

## Core Ethical Principles

### 1. Fairness

**Definition:** AI systems should ensure equitable treatment and outcomes across all stakeholders, demographics, and use cases.

**Implementation Requirements:**
- Non-discrimination based on protected characteristics
- Minimize disparate impact on vulnerable populations
- Equitable distribution of benefits
- Transparent fairness metrics and testing
- Continuous demographic auditing

**Stakeholder Responsibility:**
- Organizations: Conduct fairness testing before deployment
- Developers: Implement bias detection and mitigation
- Users: Report unfair outcomes

---

### 2. Transparency

**Definition:** AI systems and their decision-making processes should be documented, understandable, and open to scrutiny.

**Implementation Requirements:**
- Clear documentation of system design and data
- Explainability of decisions and behavior
- Disclosure of AI use to affected parties
- Reasonable access to information
- Comprehensive audit trails

**Stakeholder Responsibility:**
- Organizations: Maintain complete documentation
- Developers: Implement explainability mechanisms
- Users: Access and review information

---

### 3. Accountability

**Definition:** Clear responsibility and answerability for AI system behavior and outcomes must be established and maintained.

**Implementation Requirements:**
- Clear designation of responsibility
- Consequence frameworks for harms
- Oversight and governance mechanisms
- Performance reporting requirements
- Corrective action procedures

**Stakeholder Responsibility:**
- Organizations: Establish accountability governance
- Developers: Maintain system control and oversight
- Users: Exercise judgment on recommendations

---

### 4. Privacy

**Definition:** Personal data and sensitive information should be protected throughout AI system lifecycle.

**Implementation Requirements:**
- Data minimization and purpose limitation
- Access controls and encryption
- User rights and consent mechanisms
- Breach notification procedures
- Privacy-by-design implementation

**Stakeholder Responsibility:**
- Organizations: Implement privacy safeguards
- Developers: Build technical privacy protections
- Users: Exercise data rights

---

### 5. Benefit

**Definition:** AI systems should contribute positively to individual wellbeing and societal benefit.

**Implementation Requirements:**
- Address genuine needs with measurable benefits
- Identify and mitigate potential harms
- Distribute benefits broadly
- Assess community impacts
- Create sustainable value

**Stakeholder Responsibility:**
- Organizations: Conduct impact assessments
- Developers: Design for beneficial outcomes
- Users: Evaluate whether systems serve needs

---

## Implementation Framework

### Assessment Process

Before deploying an AI system:

1. **Identify affected parties** – Who is impacted?
2. **Assess against principles** – How does the system align?
3. **Document findings** – Record assessment results
4. **Engage stakeholders** – Seek input from affected parties
5. **Implement safeguards** – Address identified risks
6. **Establish monitoring** – Create ongoing oversight

---

## Ethical Conflict Resolution

When principles conflict, stakeholders should:

1. Document the conflict clearly
2. Consult affected stakeholders
3. Apply proportionality in balancing interests
4. Prioritize harm prevention
5. Ensure transparency in resolution
6. Record decisions for accountability

---

This ethical framework is subject to:

- Stakeholder input and feedback
- Evidence-based updates

- Transparent versioning and communication
